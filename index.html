About Kosovo

Pristina, the capital city of Kosovo © 2025 Edelweiss Air AG

Kosovo is a small country situated in the Western Balkans, bordered by Serbia, North Macedonia, Albania and Montenegro. Despite its relatively small size, it features a geographically diverse landscape composed of mountain ranges, fertile agricultural plains, and rapidly expanding urban centers, particularly in the eastern and central regions. With a population of approximately 1.8 million inhabitants, the country has experienced substantial demographic and urban growth in recent decades. Cities such as Pristina, Mitrovica, and Prizren have become densely populated and increasingly industrialized, contributing to growing environmental pressures, most notably concerning air quality.

Air pollution has emerged as a major environmental and public health concern in Kosovo. Primary pollutants, including nitrogen dioxide (NO₂) and fine particulate matter (PM₂.₅), are largely emitted from vehicular traffic, industrial processes, residential heating and coal-based energy production. These pollutants are associated with significant health risks, including respiratory and cardiovascular diseases, and their concentrations often exceed internationally recommended thresholds, particularly in urban areas.

Data Resources

Copernicus

Copernicus is the European Union’s Earth observation programme providing global, open-access data on the environment and climate.

Copernicus Atmosphere Monitoring Service

CAMS delivers continuous data on atmospheric composition, including pollutants such as NO₂ and PM₂.₅, used for air quality analysis.

Copernicus Climate Change Service

It provides data and tools to monitor and understand climate trends, supporting long-term environmental planning and assessment.

WorldPop

WorldPop offers high-resolution, gridded population data used to assess human exposure and spatial distribution across Kosovo.
